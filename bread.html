Simple FWSY Bread

Hello! Welcome to the recipe for a basic "FWSY" or "Flour Water Salt Yeast" bread. This is probably the simplest bread one can possibly make. I'm trying to keep this as simple and equipment light as possible so I'm going to list the things you absolutely need (like an oven and flour) and then a few extra things that you might want (like a bench knife). The extra things are all about making working with dough simpler and getting your results more consistent. Don't run out and buy everything on the list, just start with what you've got and maybe pick up things when you get frustrated with some part of your process!

One last thing before we start: I'd love it if you send me a picture of your completed bread or your steps! Feel free to @ me on twitter with your results or woes!

Equipment

These are the necessary pieces you'll need.

Big Bowl
You need this to mix the ingredients and then to leave in to allow the dough to proof. Ideally its about twice the size of your dough.
Mixing spoon
I prefer a hard wooden spoon to mix the dough. The recipe below is pretty tough to work with, so something strong will be needed.
Dutch Oven
This will be our baking vessel. Dutch ovens work amazingly well because you can create a good "steam-y" environment for the first half of baking. This is fairly difficult to obtain in your typical home oven, but you can work around with some oven steam hacks. I'll cover those at some point. A cast iron pan or baking tray or such will do.
Soft Towel
This is just to cover the bowl during rise / proofing. If you let it free to the air (or in the fridge) the dough will dry out. A towel with a little water on it is your best bet for bowels without tops, but saran wrap done tightly will work as well.
Hands
Kneading is hard work! Take it slow, and don't tax yourself. I mention this primarily for people with arthritis or other problems might have trouble. There are some pretty good techniques for dealing with this that I will try to write down at some point.
Counter space
This one is tricky for us with small apartments but you'll need about 4 by 3 feet square counter space. You might want to get a good large cutting board if your counters are sketchy.
Oven
To cook the bread in. You'll need something that can hit ~450F. Oven temperature consistency is often the source of a lot of problems. You might want to get a oven thermometer to double check your oven's temp.

That is it! You can totally bake bread with the above equipment. Below I've listed some extra things that will make your life much easier.

Kitchen Scale
I hesitate to put this under "optional" because honestly if you want to get consistent results you have to get a scale. If you have one, try weighing 4 cups of flour a few times. Results will vary wildly!
Bench Knife
When working with wetter doughs than we are working with here a bench knife makes it easier to work the dough and clean up.
Bowl Scraper
A little piece of rounded plastic that makes it easy to get into all the corners of a bowl or bin. Super useful and super cheap.
"Lame"
This tool has the worst name. This is essentially a razor blade attached to a stick. They are used to score the bread and make those pretty ears on a baguette. Ears allow air to escape in a controlled way and prevent blowouts. A (very) sharp knife can substitute.
Proofing Box
I use a plastic food safe container with a lid. Easy to clean and frees up your bowls. Plus the lid means long ferments don't dry out your bread!

Ingredients

  • 20oz Unbleached Bread Flour, preferably above 12.5% protein content
  • 12oz Water, roughly 110F or "warm" tapwater
  • .5oz Salt
  • .36oz Active Dry Yeast
